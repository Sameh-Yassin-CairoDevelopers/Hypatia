# Purpose

The Grammar Card represents a single grammatical rule recognized within the Hypatia Repository.

Unlike a dictionary entry, a Grammar Card describes **how language behaves**, not what a word means.

It serves as the formal representation of Egyptian grammar.

---

# Philosophy

Words are vocabulary.

Grammar is behavior.

A language cannot be reconstructed from words alone.

It must preserve the rules governing those words.

---

# Definition

A Grammar Card represents one independent grammatical phenomenon.

Examples include

* Sentence patterns
* Word order
* Verb conjugation
* Noun agreement
* Pronouns
* Particles
* Determinatives
* Relative clauses
* Genitive constructions
* Passive forms
* Negation
* Emphasis
* Questions
* Temporal constructions

Every rule has its own identity.

# Academic Notes

Unlike traditional grammars that are published as books or chapters, Hypatia decomposes grammar into **atomic grammatical knowledge objects**.

Each rule becomes independently identifiable, reviewable, versioned, referenced, and connected to stories, lexical items, entities, and sources.

This transforms Egyptian grammar from a static reference into a living, interconnected knowledge network.
