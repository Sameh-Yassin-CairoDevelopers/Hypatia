# Purpose

The Story Card represents a complete historical document preserved inside the Hypatia Repository.

A Story may be:

* Papyrus
* Inscription
* Stela
* Ostracon
* Temple Text
* Tomb Biography
* Administrative Record
* Letter
* Medical Text
* Mathematical Text
* Literary Work
* Legal Decree
* Religious Composition
* Scientific Treatise

or any future textual source.

---

# Philosophy

The Story is not merely text.

It is the historical environment in which knowledge exists.

Lexical Cards live inside Stories.

Entities appear inside Stories.

Grammar is observed inside Stories.

Meaning emerges inside Stories.

---

# Definition

A Story Card represents one historical textual artifact.

It preserves the document as a scientific object rather than merely storing its translation.

# Founder Statement

A civilization is remembered not by isolated words,

but by the stories through which those words acquired meaning.

The Story Card therefore preserves not merely text,

but the historical environment in which language lived.

By treating every historical document as an independent Knowledge Object,

Hypatia preserves the documentary memory of ancient civilizations while allowing every lexical, grammatical, and semantic layer to evolve without ever altering the original source.

---

# Academic Notes

Unlike traditional corpora where a document is often treated as plain text, the Story Card models a document as a **structured historical knowledge object**.

Its primary role is to serve as the central meeting point between:

* Lexical Cards,
* Entity Cards,
* Grammar Cards,
* Source Cards,
* Domain Cards,
* Knowledge Graph,
* and AI Training.

This architecture ensures that every linguistic observation remains permanently traceable to its documentary evidence.
