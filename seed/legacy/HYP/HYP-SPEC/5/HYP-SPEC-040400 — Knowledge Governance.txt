# Purpose

The Knowledge Governance System defines the institutional framework responsible for protecting, managing, and evolving the scientific knowledge contained within the Hypatia Repository.

Its objective is to ensure that every decision affecting the Repository is transparent, accountable, reproducible, and scientifically justified.

Knowledge Governance governs the Repository.

It does not govern individuals.

---

# Philosophy

Repositories outlive founders.

Institutions outlive repositories.

Governance ensures that knowledge survives both.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Authority belongs to evidence.

Responsibility belongs to governance.

No individual permanently owns scientific truth.

---

# Governance Objectives

The Governance System exists to

* preserve Repository integrity.
* protect scientific neutrality.
* prevent arbitrary modification.
* ensure transparent decision making.
* maintain historical accountability.
* support long-term institutional continuity.

---

# Governance Structure

The governance hierarchy is composed of successive institutional layers.

---

## Level 1

Founder Governance

Applicable during the Founder Edition.

The Founder retains final authority over

* Repository architecture
* Scientific direction
* Structural changes
* Governance evolution

The Founder is accountable to the documented principles of Hypatia.

---

## Level 2

Scientific Review

Reviewers evaluate proposals.

Their authority extends only to scientific evaluation.

Reviewers cannot alter governance rules.

---

## Level 3

Academic Validation

Validators certify scientific quality.

Certification grants publication eligibility.

Validators do not modify Repository architecture.

---

## Level 4

Community Governance

Introduced in future editions.

The community participates through structured governance mechanisms while respecting Repository standards.

# Governance Motto

# Founder Statement

Hypatia was founded by individuals.

It is intended to endure as an institution.

For that reason, governance is not designed to concentrate authority, but to distribute responsibility while preserving scientific rigor.

As the project grows beyond its founder, governance becomes the mechanism through which the original principles continue to guide future generations without preventing legitimate scientific evolution.

---

# Academic Notes

The Knowledge Governance System distinguishes **institutional authority** from **scientific authority**.

Institutional authority defines how decisions are made.

Scientific authority determines whether evidence supports knowledge.

By separating these two dimensions, Hypatia preserves methodological integrity while allowing both its governance model and its scientific corpus to evolve independently over time.
