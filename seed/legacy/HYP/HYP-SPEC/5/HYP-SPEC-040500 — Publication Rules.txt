# Purpose

The Publication Rules define the criteria, procedures, and governance governing the publication of scientific knowledge through the Hypatia platform.

Publication represents the final stage of the scientific lifecycle.

Only validated knowledge becomes publicly accessible.

---

# Philosophy

Every published statement represents the scientific position of the Repository at a specific revision.

Publication is therefore an act of scientific responsibility.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Publication never creates knowledge.

Publication reveals validated knowledge.

---

# Publication Workflow

```text id="publication_workflow"
Proposal

↓

Scientific Review

↓

Academic Validation

↓

Repository Revision

↓

Publication Approval

↓

Public Release
```

---

# Publication Eligibility

A Knowledge Object may be published only if

* Proposal accepted.
* Scientific review completed.
* Academic validation completed.
* Repository revision finalized.
* Required metadata complete.
* Publication approval granted.

Failure of any requirement blocks publication.

---

# Publication Scope

Publication applies to

* Dictionary Cards
* Entity Cards
* Story Cards
* Grammar Cards
* Relation Cards
* Domain Cards
* Source Cards
* Knowledge Graph
* Search Services
* Website
* Public API
* AI Inference Packages

---

# Publication Levels

Knowledge is published according to its certification level.

---

## Official

Fully validated scientific knowledge.

Visible by default.

---

## Provisional

Scientifically useful.

Contains documented uncertainty.

Clearly marked.

---

## Experimental

Available for research purposes.

Hidden from normal users.

Accessible only through research interfaces.

---

## Internal

Repository only.

Never published.

---

## Archived

Historically preserved.

Not considered current knowledge.

Remains permanently accessible.

# AI Position

Artificial Intelligence consumes only published knowledge unless explicitly operating in research mode.

Unpublished Repository Objects remain inaccessible to production AI services.

---

# Community Position

Community members view only knowledge appropriate to the publication level granted by governance.

Research contributors may receive expanded visibility according to institutional policy.

---

# Long-Term Objective

The Publication Rules establish Hypatia as a trustworthy scientific publisher whose public knowledge remains reproducible, evidence-based, versioned, and permanently citable.

Every published object becomes a stable scholarly reference while preserving complete historical continuity.

---

# Scientific Law

Validation creates trust.

Publication creates accessibility.

History preserves both.

---

# Publication Motto

> **Only Verified Knowledge Becomes Public Knowledge.**

---

# Founder Statement

Publishing is not the conclusion of scientific work.

It is the beginning of scientific responsibility.

Every published object represents a commitment by the Hypatia Repository to the standards of evidence, transparency, and reproducibility established throughout its governance framework.

Future discoveries may refine published knowledge, but they will never erase the historical record of what was once understood.

In this way, Hypatia preserves both the evolution of knowledge and the integrity of scientific publication.

---

# Academic Notes

The Publication Rules complete the governance architecture by separating **knowledge creation**, **knowledge validation**, and **knowledge dissemination** into independent institutional stages.

This separation ensures that public-facing content reflects only scientifically certified Repository revisions while preserving the complete internal history of scholarly debate, rejected hypotheses, provisional interpretations, and archived publications.
