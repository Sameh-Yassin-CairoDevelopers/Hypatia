# Purpose

The Review System defines the scientific review process governing every proposed modification to the Hypatia Repository.

Its objective is to preserve scientific integrity, ensure transparency, maintain historical traceability, and guarantee that every accepted change is supported by verifiable evidence.

---

# Philosophy

Knowledge is not accepted because it is proposed.

Knowledge is accepted because it withstands scientific review.

The Review System therefore protects the Repository from opinion, speculation, and accidental corruption.

---

# Responsibilities

The Review System is responsible for

* Assigning reviewers.
* Managing scientific discussions.
* Recording reviewer decisions.
* Tracking review history.
* Approving or rejecting proposals.
* Preserving review evidence.
* Initiating Repository revisions.

It never edits Repository Objects directly.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Every Repository modification requires review.

No exceptions.

# Review Stages

## Stage 1

Submission

The proposal is formally registered.

---

## Stage 2

Validation

Structural completeness is verified.

Mandatory information is checked.

Supporting evidence is confirmed.

---

## Stage 3

Reviewer Assignment

Qualified reviewers are assigned.

Assignment rules depend on

* domain
* expertise
* workload
* conflict of interest

---

## Stage 4

Scientific Evaluation

Reviewers examine

* evidence
* references
* consistency
* historical accuracy
* linguistic correctness

---

## Stage 5

Decision

Possible outcomes

```text id="xoz5fz"
Accept

Reject

Needs Revision

Merge

Postpone
```

---

## Stage 6

Repository Revision

Accepted proposals generate a Repository Revision.

Rejected proposals remain archived.

# Reviewer Ethics

Every reviewer must

* act objectively
* disclose conflicts of interest
* justify decisions
* preserve scientific neutrality

# AI Position

* detecting inconsistencies

# Founder Statement

Scientific knowledge gains authority through scrutiny, not through authorship.

The Review System ensures that every contribution—whether submitted by the founder, a researcher, or the future community—is evaluated according to the same scientific standards.

By preserving every discussion, every justification, and every decision, Hypatia protects not only its knowledge, but also the integrity of the process through which that knowledge becomes trusted.

---

# Academic Notes

The Review System intentionally separates **scientific evaluation** from **repository modification**.

Reviewers determine whether evidence supports a proposal.

Only after a documented decision does the Repository evolve through a formal revision.

This separation guarantees reproducibility, institutional accountability, and long-term scholarly credibility.
