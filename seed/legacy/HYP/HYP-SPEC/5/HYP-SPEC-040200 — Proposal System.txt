---

# Purpose

The Proposal System defines the official mechanism through which new knowledge, corrections, enhancements, and scientific discussions enter the Hypatia ecosystem.

Every modification begins as a Proposal.

Every Proposal becomes part of the permanent scientific history of the Repository.

---

# Philosophy

Knowledge evolves through proposals.

Proposals evolve through review.

Accepted proposals evolve the Repository.

Nothing enters the Repository directly.

---

# Responsibilities

The Proposal System is responsible for

* Creating proposals.
* Registering scientific changes.
* Tracking proposal history.
* Managing proposal revisions.
* Linking supporting evidence.
* Connecting proposals to Repository Objects.
* Delivering proposals to the Review System.

It never modifies Repository Objects.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Every Repository change begins as a Proposal.

Every Proposal receives a permanent identity.

# Founder Statement

Scientific progress begins with questions, observations, and hypotheses.

Not every proposal will become accepted knowledge.

Yet every proposal contributes to the intellectual history of the project.

By preserving proposals alongside the discussions and evidence that surround them, Hypatia documents not only what became knowledge, but also how scientific understanding evolved over time.

---

# Academic Notes

The Proposal System separates **scientific contribution** from **scientific acceptance**.

This distinction allows the Repository to preserve the complete evolution of ideas while maintaining the integrity of the accepted knowledge base.

Every proposal remains a citable historical artifact, ensuring transparency, accountability, and reproducibility throughout the lifetime of the Hypatia project.
