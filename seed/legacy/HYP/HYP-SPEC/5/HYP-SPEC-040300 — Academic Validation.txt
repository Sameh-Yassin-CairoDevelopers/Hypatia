# Purpose

The Academic Validation System defines the scientific criteria required for knowledge to become an official part of the Hypatia Repository.

Its purpose is to ensure that every accepted Knowledge Object satisfies objective academic standards before publication.

Academic Validation is the final scientific checkpoint preceding Repository integration.

---

# Philosophy

Review evaluates proposals.

Academic Validation certifies knowledge.

Acceptance is procedural.

Validation is scientific.

---

# Responsibilities

The Academic Validation System is responsible for

* Verifying scientific evidence.
* Assessing source quality.
* Confirming scholarly consistency.
* Measuring confidence.
* Detecting unresolved conflicts.
* Certifying Repository readiness.

It never edits Repository Objects.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Knowledge is considered official only after successful academic validation.

Repository acceptance alone is insufficient.

# Validation Criteria

Every Knowledge Object is evaluated according to the following dimensions.

---

## Scientific Evidence

The object must be supported by identifiable evidence.

Examples

* Primary inscriptions
* Published editions
* Museum records
* Academic publications
* Verified repository sources

---

## Source Reliability

Referenced sources are evaluated for academic reliability.

Possible categories

```text id="xbr3tl"
Primary Source

Secondary Source

Scholarly Publication

Reference Work

Repository Internal Source
```

---

## Internal Consistency

The object must not contradict validated Repository knowledge without documented justification.

Conflicts are recorded for review.

---

## Historical Consistency

Historical claims are evaluated against accepted chronology, geography, and documented evidence.

---

## Linguistic Consistency

Applicable to language-related objects.

Validation includes

* transliteration
* grammar
* lexical usage
* orthographic consistency

---

## Semantic Consistency

Relationships must remain coherent within the Knowledge Graph.

Broken semantic links invalidate certification.

---

# Validation Levels

Each object receives one validation level.

---

## Level A

Fully Validated

Meets all academic requirements.

Suitable for publication.

---

## Level B

Validated with Reservations

Scientifically acceptable.

Minor uncertainty documented.

---

## Level C

Provisional

Evidence exists.

Further confirmation required.

Visible with appropriate indication.

---

## Level D

Unverified

Insufficient evidence.

Excluded from official publication.

---

## Level E

Rejected

Scientifically unsupported.

Archived.

* Repository revisions affect related objects

# Validator Responsibilities

Validators must

* remain scientifically objective
* document every decision
* reference supporting evidence
* declare uncertainty when necessary
* avoid unsupported conclusions

---

# AI Position

Artificial Intelligence may assist validation by

* locating references
* detecting inconsistencies
* comparing parallel sources

AI never certifies knowledge.

Certification remains exclusively a human academic responsibility.

---

# Community Position

Community contributions may reach Academic Validation only after successful scientific review.

Validation standards are identical for all contributors.

# Long-Term Objective

The Academic Validation System establishes Hypatia as a scientifically curated knowledge institution whose published content satisfies transparent, reproducible, and evidence-based academic standards.

---

# Scientific Law

Evidence creates confidence.

Confidence enables validation.

Validation creates knowledge.

# Founder Statement

The value of a scientific repository is determined not by the quantity of its knowledge, but by the reliability of that knowledge.

Academic Validation protects Hypatia from uncertainty becoming fact.

Every certified object represents a documented agreement between evidence, scholarly reasoning, and institutional standards.

In doing so, Hypatia preserves not only information, but academic trust itself.

---

# Academic Notes

The Academic Validation System intentionally distinguishes **procedural acceptance** from **scientific certification**.

A proposal may successfully complete the review process yet remain provisional until sufficient evidence supports its publication.

This additional validation layer ensures that Hypatia maintains the methodological rigor expected of long-term scholarly repositories while remaining open to future discoveries and reinterpretations.
