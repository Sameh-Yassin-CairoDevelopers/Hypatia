# Purpose

This guide defines the backup architecture, preservation strategy, recovery procedures, and operational standards protecting every engineering and scientific asset within the Hypatia Platform.

Backups ensure that no verified scientific knowledge, engineering artifact, or operational history is ever permanently lost.

---

# Philosophy

Knowledge is irreplaceable.

Backups protect civilization.

Recovery preserves continuity.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Every critical asset must exist in at least three independent copies.

One operational.

One local backup.

One off-site backup.

---

```text id="backup_architecture"

        ▼
```

Each layer is independently recoverable.

# Backup Types

---

## Incremental Backup

Stores only changes.

Used for frequent protection.

---

## Differential Backup

Stores changes since the previous full backup.

Supports efficient recovery.

---

## Full Backup

Captures the complete platform.

Required before every major migration.

---

## Archive Backup

Creates immutable long-term historical snapshots.

Archive backups are never overwritten.

---

Every backup includes

```text id="backup_naming"

```

# Backup Storage

Backups should be distributed across

* production storage
* local infrastructure
* institutional archive
* geographically separate storage

No single storage location is considered sufficient.

---

# Encryption

All backups containing sensitive information must be encrypted.

Encryption keys are managed independently of backup storage.

---

# Verification

Every backup undergoes

* checksum verification
* integrity validation
* restoration simulation

Unverified backups are considered invalid.

---

# Academic Notes

The Hypatia Backup Strategy adopts a **Knowledge Preservation Backup Model (KPBM)**.

Within KPBM, backup operations protect not only operational assets but the complete scientific state of the platform, including Repository revisions, Builder ecosystem, AI evolution, governance history, and engineering documentation.

By treating institutional knowledge as the primary preservation target, KPBM extends conventional backup methodologies into a comprehensive long-term scientific continuity framework.

# ✦ Founder Insight

أقترح أن نضيف داخل **Hypatia Builder Framework** مكونًا جديدًا يسمى:

## **Preservation Builder**

وليس **Backup Builder** فقط.

لأن مهمته لن تكون مجرد إنشاء نسخ احتياطية، بل:

* إنشاء النسخ الاحتياطية.
* التحقق من سلامتها.
* تنفيذ اختبارات استرجاع دورية.
* مراقبة أعمار النسخ.
* إنشاء أرشيفات سنوية غير قابلة للتعديل (Immutable Archives).
* إصدار **Preservation Health Report** يوضح مدى أمان ذاكرة المنصة.

وبذلك يتحول النسخ الاحتياطى من عملية تشغيلية إلى **منظومة علمية لحفظ الذاكرة المؤسسية**، وهو ما يتوافق مع رسالة هيباتيا فى حفظ المعرفة للأجيال القادمة.
