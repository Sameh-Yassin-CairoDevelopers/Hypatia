# Purpose

This document defines the philosophical foundations governing how Artificial Intelligence is trained within the Hypatia ecosystem.

Its purpose is to ensure that every training process remains scientifically grounded, reproducible, explainable, and permanently connected to the Repository.

Training is considered a scientific activity rather than a computational procedure.

---

# Philosophy

Artificial Intelligence does not memorize knowledge.

It learns patterns from scientifically curated knowledge.

The Repository remains the source of truth.

The model learns from the Repository.

The Repository never learns from the model.

---

# Fundamental Principle

The objective of training is **understanding**, not memorization.

A model capable of explaining why an answer is likely is preferred over a model that merely predicts the correct answer.

---

# Scientific Principles

Training follows seven foundational principles.

---

## Principle 1

Repository Before Model

The Repository always exists before any neural model.

Training datasets are generated from Repository revisions.

No model trains directly on raw sources.

---

## Principle 2

Knowledge Before Statistics

Statistical optimization must never replace scientific correctness.

The Repository determines what is correct.

Training determines how to approximate that knowledge.

---

## Principle 3

Explainability Before Accuracy

An explainable prediction with slightly lower confidence is preferable to an opaque prediction with marginally higher accuracy.

Hypatia prioritizes interpretable intelligence.

---

## Principle 4

Generalization Before Memorization

The model should learn linguistic structure, semantic relationships, and historical patterns rather than memorize isolated examples.

Training emphasizes transferable understanding.

---

## Principle 5

Evidence Before Confidence

Confidence scores must emerge from evidence-rich training.

Confidence is never treated as certainty.

Higher confidence reflects stronger support, not absolute truth.

---

## Principle 6

Continuous Learning Through Repository Evolution

The model evolves because the Repository evolves.

Retraining is triggered by Repository revisions rather than arbitrary schedules.

---

## Principle 7

Knowledge Independence

The Repository must remain scientifically valuable even if no trained model exists.

Training never becomes the primary knowledge store.

---

Errors are scientific observations.

Every prediction error is treated as an opportunity to improve

* Repository quality
* Dataset quality
* Resolver quality
* Training strategy

Errors are never discarded.

# Human-in-the-Loop

The trained model represents a probabilistic approximation of Repository knowledge.

The Repository remains authoritative.

# Founder Statement

Hypatia is not being trained to become another language model.

It is being trained to become a scholarly companion capable of navigating one of humanity's oldest written traditions.

Every parameter learned by the model ultimately derives from knowledge curated, reviewed, validated, and preserved within the Repository.

As the Repository matures, the intelligence matures with it.

In this philosophy, scientific knowledge is permanent; neural intelligence is evolutionary.

---

# Academic Notes

The Hypatia Training Philosophy adopts a **Knowledge-Centric Learning Paradigm**, in which supervised learning is viewed as the computational interpretation of an independently governed scientific knowledge base.

Unlike conventional end-to-end language models that implicitly absorb both facts and reasoning into their parameters, Hypatia explicitly separates **knowledge governance** from **statistical learning**. This distinction improves reproducibility, explainability, long-term maintainability, and scientific trust, while allowing successive generations of models to inherit the same curated intellectual foundation.
