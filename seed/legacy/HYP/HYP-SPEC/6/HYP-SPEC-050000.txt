# Purpose

This document defines the overall Artificial Intelligence architecture of the Hypatia Platform.

It establishes the relationship between the scientific Repository, the Knowledge Graph, the Dataset Builders, and the future Hypatia AI models.

The objective is to ensure that Artificial Intelligence remains a consumer of scientific knowledge rather than its source.

---

# Philosophy

Knowledge precedes Intelligence.

Artificial Intelligence does not create knowledge.

It learns from knowledge.

Knowledge remains independent from every model.

Models may evolve.

The Repository must remain permanent.

---

# Architectural Principle

Hypatia separates

**Knowledge**

from

**Artificial Intelligence**

These two systems evolve independently.

# Core Components

The AI ecosystem consists of six independent layers.

---

## Layer 1

Scientific Repository

The Repository is the permanent scientific memory.

It contains

* Dictionary Cards
* Entity Cards
* Story Cards
* Grammar Cards
* Relations
* Sources
* Domains
* Metadata

No AI model modifies the Repository.

---

## Layer 2

Knowledge Graph

The Knowledge Graph organizes Repository Objects into a semantic network.

It represents

* relationships
* context
* hierarchy
* historical connections

The graph is regenerated from the Repository.

---

## Layer 3

Dataset Builders

Two independent builders exist.

### Training Dataset Builder

Produces datasets for machine learning.

### Inference Dataset Builder

Produces optimized knowledge packages for retrieval and reasoning.

Datasets never replace the Repository.

---

## Layer 4

Artificial Intelligence

AI models learn exclusively from generated datasets.

They never train directly on Repository Objects.

---

## Layer 5

Reasoning Layer

Future reasoning systems consume

* Repository
* Knowledge Graph
* Inference Datasets

They may combine symbolic reasoning with neural inference.

---

## Layer 6

Applications

Applications include

* Website
* Public API
* AI Assistant
* Research Tools
* Future Desktop Clients
* Future Mobile Applications

Applications consume knowledge.

They never redefine it.

# Founder Statement

Most Artificial Intelligence projects begin by building a model.

Hypatia begins by building a civilization of knowledge.

The Repository is designed to remain scientifically meaningful even if every neural network disappears tomorrow.

Conversely, future generations of AI may change completely while continuing to learn from the same Repository without requiring its reconstruction.

In this architecture, Artificial Intelligence becomes a powerful interpreter of knowledge—not its owner, author, or ultimate authority.

# Academic Notes

The Hypatia AI Architecture adopts a **Knowledge-Centric Artificial Intelligence (KCAI)** paradigm.

Unlike conventional AI systems in which knowledge is implicitly embedded within model parameters, Hypatia preserves explicit, versioned, and independently governed scientific knowledge in a permanent Repository.

Neural models operate as interchangeable computational layers positioned above this Repository, enabling reproducibility, explainability, symbolic integration, Retrieval-Augmented Generation, and long-term institutional sustainability.

This separation establishes Hypatia not merely as an AI application, but as a durable scientific knowledge infrastructure capable of supporting successive generations of artificial intelligence technologies without compromising the integrity of its scholarly foundation.
