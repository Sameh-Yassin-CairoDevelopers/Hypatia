# Purpose

The Feedback Pipeline manages the complete lifecycle of scientific proposals submitted by founders, reviewers, researchers, and the future community.

Its responsibility is to ensure that every accepted improvement becomes part of the Hypatia ecosystem through a transparent, traceable, and reproducible scientific process.

---

# Philosophy

Knowledge grows through review.

Not through replacement.

Every proposal is evidence.

Every decision is recorded.

Nothing disappears.

---

# Responsibilities

The Feedback Pipeline is responsible for

* Receiving proposals.
* Registering suggestions.
* Classifying proposal types.
* Assigning reviewers.
* Recording discussions.
* Managing approval workflow.
* Triggering Repository revisions.

It never edits Repository Objects directly.

# Validation

Every proposal is validated before review.

Validation includes

* Structural correctness
* Mandatory information
* Supporting evidence
* Reference verification
* Duplicate proposal detection

Invalid proposals are returned for revision.

---

# Scientific Review

Every proposal undergoes scientific evaluation.

Reviewers may

* Accept
* Reject
* Merge
* Request Revision

Every decision is documented.

---

# Evidence Requirement

Every proposal must include

* Scientific justification
* Supporting references
* Optional examples
* Author notes

Unsupported proposals cannot be accepted.

---

# Founder Statement

No scientific work is ever truly complete.

Every generation discovers new evidence, proposes new interpretations, and refines previous understanding.

The Feedback Pipeline exists to ensure that Hypatia welcomes this continuous evolution without sacrificing consistency, transparency, or historical accountability.

By recording every proposal, every discussion, and every decision, Hypatia preserves not only knowledge itself, but also the scientific process through which that knowledge evolved.

---

# Academic Notes

The Feedback Pipeline separates **knowledge contribution** from **knowledge acceptance**.

This distinction guarantees that repository growth remains governed by evidence rather than authority, while preserving a complete audit trail of every proposal, accepted or rejected.

As the Hypatia community expands, this pipeline provides the governance framework necessary to maintain long-term scientific reliability without limiting collaborative development.
