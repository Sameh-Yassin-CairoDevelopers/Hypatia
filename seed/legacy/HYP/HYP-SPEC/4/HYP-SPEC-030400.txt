# HYP-SPEC-030400

# Resolver Pipeline Specification

**Project:** Hypatia Knowledge Platform

**Document ID:** HYP-SPEC-030400

**Version:** 1.0 Founder Edition

**Status:** Official

**Classification:** Core Pipeline

---

# Purpose

The Resolver Pipeline transforms validated Knowledge Objects into semantically connected knowledge.

Its mission is to identify relationships, resolve ambiguity, enrich context, and prepare objects for Knowledge Graph construction.

The Resolver Pipeline is the intelligence layer of Hypatia.

---

# Philosophy

Validation ensures that knowledge is structurally correct.

Resolution ensures that knowledge becomes understandable.

Resolvers do not create knowledge.

Resolvers discover how knowledge is connected.

---

# Pipeline Position

```text
Validated Package
        │
        ▼
Resolver Pipeline
        │
        ▼
Resolved Knowledge
        │
        ▼
Knowledge Graph Builder
```

---

# Responsibilities

The Resolver Pipeline is responsible for

* Resolving identities.
* Resolving ambiguity.
* Linking knowledge objects.
* Assigning semantic context.
* Ranking candidate meanings.
* Recording evidence.
* Producing explainable results.

It never edits repository objects directly.

---

# Execution Model

The Resolver Pipeline operates sequentially.

Each Resolver enriches the result of the previous Resolver.

```text
Input

↓

Identity Resolution

↓

Lexical Resolution

↓

Grammar Resolution

↓

Entity Resolution

↓

Domain Resolution

↓

Story Resolution

↓

Relation Resolution

↓

Confidence Resolution

↓

Output
```

---

# Input

The Resolver Pipeline accepts validated Knowledge Objects.

Examples

* Story Cards
* Dictionary Cards
* Entity Cards
* Grammar Cards
* Source Cards
* Domain Cards

---

# Resolver Order

## Phase 1

Identity Resolution

Verifies

* object identity
* identifiers
* duplicates
* aliases

---

## Phase 2

Lexical Resolution

Determines

* canonical lemma
* lexical family
* derived forms
* possible senses

---

## Phase 3

Grammar Resolution

Determines

* grammatical role
* sentence function
* syntactic constraints
* grammatical context

---

## Phase 4

Entity Resolution

Determines

* referenced entities
* aliases
* historical identities
* entity confidence

---

## Phase 5

Domain Resolution

Determines

* scientific domains
* interdisciplinary domains
* domain confidence

---

## Phase 6

Story Resolution

Determines

* narrative context
* referenced stories
* textual similarity
* story signatures

---

## Phase 7

Relation Resolution

Discovers

* semantic relations
* historical relations
* lexical relations
* inferred links

---

## Phase 8

Confidence Resolution

Calculates

* overall confidence
* supporting evidence
* competing interpretations
* ranking

---

# Candidate Resolution

Whenever multiple interpretations exist

Resolvers produce

```text
Candidate List
```

Each candidate contains

* candidate
* confidence
* evidence
* supporting objects

No candidate is discarded.

Only ranked.

---

# Resolution Trace

Every Resolver execution generates

```text
Resolver Name

↓

Visited Objects

↓

↓

Decision

↓

Confidence
```

This trace becomes permanently available.

# Resolver Cooperation

Resolvers may invoke one another.

Example

```text

↓

↓

```

Every invocation is logged.

# Failure Handling

If one Resolver fails

The Pipeline continues.

The failure becomes part of the Resolution Trace.

Knowledge is never discarded.

# Founder Statement

Knowledge rarely arrives complete.

Ancient texts are fragmentary.

Words are ambiguous.

Names change through history.

Meanings evolve.

The Resolver Pipeline exists to reconstruct understanding without altering evidence.

It transforms isolated Knowledge Objects into coherent scientific knowledge while preserving every competing interpretation.

Its role is not to decide what is true.

Its role is to reveal every scientifically defensible possibility.

---

# Academic Notes

The Resolver Pipeline intentionally separates semantic interpretation from data storage.

Every Resolver contributes one layer of understanding without modifying the Repository itself.

This architecture enables independent evolution of linguistic models, reasoning algorithms, and AI systems while guaranteeing that all interpretations remain fully explainable, reproducible, and traceable to their supporting evidence.
