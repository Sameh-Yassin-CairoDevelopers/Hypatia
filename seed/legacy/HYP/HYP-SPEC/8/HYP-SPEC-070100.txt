# Document Status

This is a continuously evolving research roadmap.

Its purpose is not to define completed work, but to organize future scientific, engineering, and institutional research efforts.

Items may be added, reordered, merged, postponed, completed, or archived throughout the lifetime of the project.

---

# Purpose

This roadmap records the future scientific direction of Hypatia.

It provides a structured view of

* current research
* planned research
* experimental ideas
* engineering challenges
* AI evolution
* long-term institutional goals

The roadmap connects everyday engineering work with long-term scientific vision.

# Founder Statement

This roadmap is intentionally incomplete.

Its incompleteness is evidence that Hypatia remains alive.

A finished roadmap would imply that research has ended.

Hypatia is designed so that every implementation generates new questions, every new question becomes a future experiment, and every experiment expands humanity's understanding of Ancient Egyptian civilization.

---

# Academic Notes

The Hypatia Research Roadmap functions as a **Living Scientific Backlog (LSB)**.

Unlike conventional software roadmaps that conclude with feature completion, LSB continuously accumulates unanswered questions, engineering discoveries, AI opportunities, scholarly collaborations, and methodological improvements.

The roadmap therefore becomes a historical record of the intellectual evolution of the project itself.
